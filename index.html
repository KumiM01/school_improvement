School Improvement Program

Afternoon Class Teacher Program

Implementation: A stipend sourced from 'DaKota Project' revenue will be allocated to hiring retired teachers or unemployed graduates as afternoon teachers

  1. 1

    Daily recording forms (either paper or digital) will be provided to teachers, using student numbers for anonymity.

  2. 2

    A local youth team in the school's computer lab will be responsible for inputting and organizing the data, as well as maintaining the schools’ website.

  3. 3

    The recorded information will be shared with afternoon teachers, with a specific focus on the day’s weaker students and subjects.

IT Educlogo ation Program

Implementation: 'DaKota Project' funds will be utilized to train and employ youth for IT education

  1. 1

    Local youth will undergo training in IT skills.

  2. 2

    They will then be tasked with teaching all students and teachers, creating a tech-savvy school environment.

  3. 3

    Weekend classes for the school community can be organized, with a nominal fee.

  4. 4

    Compensation for the youth instructors will be provided for from the 'DaKota Project' funds.

Expected Outcomes:

  • Improved academic performance, particularly among weaker students.
  • Enhanced IT literacy across the school community.
  • Sustainable funding through the 'DaKota Project' for ongoing educational initiatives.

Budget Allocation: A percentage of 'DaKota Project' revenue will be allocated to stipends, training, and salaries for afternoon teachers and IT education

The DaKota Project's proposed initiatives aim to create a comprehensive educational environment, addressing individual learning needs, and promoting technological literacy for a brighter academic future
